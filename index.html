About the School

Artificial intelligence is reshaping how science is conducted, how innovation unfolds, and how policy is designed and evaluated. For researchers working at the intersection of science, technology, and innovation (STI), engaging critically and competently with AI is rapidly becoming essential — not only as a topic of inquiry, but as a practical research tool and a subject of governance concern.

The EU-SPRI Autumn School on Responsible AI for Science, Technology, Innovation, and Policy is a one-week intensive training programme for PhD candidates and early-career researchers (ECRs). Hosted at the Center for Innovation Research (CIRCLE) at Lund University in Lund, Sweden, the School offers a structured, hands-on introduction to AI in contemporary STI research — integrating conceptual, methodological, and ethical dimensions.

The School is jointly organized by CIRCLE, Lund University and VTT Technical Research Centre of Finland, with the participation of additional partner institutions.

Three Perspectives on AI

The programme is structured around three complementary lenses:

AI as a Research Tool

Hands-on experience with machine learning, NLP, topic modelling, semantic analysis, automated coding, LLMs, and generative AI for social science tasks. Lab sessions are designed to be accessible without prior programming experience.

AI as an Object of Inquiry

How AI technologies shape innovation processes, entrepreneurship, industrial dynamics, scientific production, and policy design — with engagement with current governance debates across regional and institutional contexts.

AI as a Socio-Technical System

The ethical, societal, and regulatory dimensions of AI: transparency, fairness, bias, sustainability, interpretability, and responsible research practice — developing the vocabulary to contribute to evidence-based policy discussions.

Programme Format

The School combines lectures, methodological workshops, case discussions, peer learning, and collaborative project work. A dedicated methods and lab track equips participants with practical tools — from data collection and preprocessing to model evaluation and communicating AI-derived insights responsibly. Participants also receive feedback on their own ongoing research from senior scholars and build connections with peers across institutions and disciplines.

One day of the programme is organised as a joint event with CIRCLE and the broader Lund University research community, offering additional opportunities for engagement and collaboration.

Group Research Project: Working in interdisciplinary groups of five, participants collaboratively develop a short research proposal applying AI to an STI challenge. On the final day, proposals are evaluated by a jury of senior scholars and practitioners — simulating a real-world funding panel and providing formative feedback that participants can build on in future applications.

Who Should Apply

The School welcomes applications from PhD students and early-career researchers from all disciplines with an interest in AI and its intersections with science, technology, and innovation. We actively seek a diverse cohort — both quantitative and qualitative researchers, and both those with and without prior technical experience in AI. Prior coding or AI skills are not required. What matters is motivation to engage with these topics and a willingness to learn.

ECRs should be within five years of PhD graduation. All applicants must be registered in a doctoral programme or hold a completed PhD. Applications are welcome regardless of nationality or country of origin. The programme is conducted in English.

What Participants Receive

  • Introduction to AI methods for empirical STI research
  • Conceptual grounding in AI governance, ethics, and innovation theory
  • Hands-on lab sessions with computational tools
  • Feedback from senior scholars on your own research project
  • A collaborative group research proposal as a concrete output
  • Access to the CIRCLE research environment and network
  • Networking and mentorship with international faculty and peers
  • Foundation for future grant applications and collaborations

How to Apply

To apply, please prepare and submit the following three documents:

  1. Application statement — describing your motivation for attending, how the School relates to your current research and career development, and an overview of your research project and data sources
  2. CV — current curriculum vitae
  3. Writing sample — an example of academic writing

Application deadline: 1 August 2026

Applicants will be notified with sufficient time to make travel arrangements. For questions, please contact the organising team at CIRCLE, Lund University.
